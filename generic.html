USAID Under Scrutiny Amid Allegations of Aiding Terrorists Group.

Boko Haram

The united state further condemns the claims..

Allegations have surfaced, claiming that the United States Agency for International Development (USAID) has inadvertently funneled millions to terrorist organizations worldwide. Nigeria's Minister of Foreign Affairs, Yusuf Tuggar, responded to the claims, stating that if true, it would be a betrayal of trust.

The allegations have raised concerns about Nigeria's notorious Boko Haram terrorist group. For over a decade, Boko Haram has ravaged Nigeria's northeast, killing tens of thousands and targeting police, military, and civilian populations.

US Congressman Scott Perry has asserted that USAID has been financing terrorist groups, including Boko Haram, with $697 million annually. Perry also mentioned that shipments of cash have been sent to Madrasas, ISIS, Al-Qaeda, and other terrorist training camps.

The US Mission to Nigeria has responded to the allegations, emphasizing that comprehensive monitoring and evaluation systems are in place to ensure that US assistance reaches its intended recipients. The mission also highlighted that Boko Haram was designated as a Foreign Terrorist Organization (FTO) by the US Secretary of State in 2013.
